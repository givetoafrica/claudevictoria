# Episode 1 — What if it Rained Glass... Sideways? (FINAL)

Channel: Better Than Fiction · Format: locked style (see data/script-style-guide.md)
Subject: HD 189733b — "the Glass Storm Planet" · Runtime ~4:00
Cast: Narrator (percy voice) · Explorer (sparky voice) · Rex the robot (prof voice)

---

**NARRATOR:** What if it rained glass... sideways?

**REX:** Sideways rain? No thank you. My umbrella only works up and down.

**EXPLORER:** Rex, guess what! This is not a what-if. This planet is REAL!

**NARRATOR:** Yes! Far, far away in space, there is a real planet where this
happens. Scientists gave it a very boring name. So we will call it... the
Glass Storm Planet. Ready? Let's go!

**NARRATOR:** Firstly — from far away, this planet looks just like Earth. It
is big, round, and beautiful blue. Like a giant marble floating in space.

**EXPLORER:** Blue! So it has big oceans? Can we go swimming?

**NARRATOR:** No swimming! There is no water AT ALL. This planet is hotter
than a pizza oven. Hotter than a hundred pizza ovens!

**REX:** Mmm. Pizza.

**NARRATOR:** So why is it blue? Here comes the amazing part. Its clouds are
not made of fluffy water like ours. Its clouds are made of melted rock —
tiny, tiny bits of GLASS, floating in the sky! And all that glass shines
blue. The planet is blue because its sky is full of glass!

**REX:** So the planet is tricking us. It looks friendly. It is NOT friendly.

**NARRATOR:** Secondly — the wind. Think of the fastest race car. This wind
is faster. Think of the fastest airplane. This wind is faster! The wind on
this planet is even faster than SOUND. If you shouted "hello," the wind
would fly away with your hello before anyone could hear it!

**REX:** Hello? Hello?? Where did my hello go?!

**NARRATOR:** Thirdly — why is it so windy? This planet never turns around.
One side always looks at its sun. That side gets hotter and hotter and
hotter. The other side is always dark and night. All that hot air wants to
run to the cold side. So the air runs around the planet — whooooosh — and
it never, ever stops.

**EXPLORER:** A wind that never stops? Ever? Not even for a nap?

**NARRATOR:** Never! And fourthly — now put it all together. Glass in the
sky. Wind faster than sound. On Earth, rain falls down. Plop, plop, plop.
But on the Glass Storm Planet, the wind is TOO fast. The rain never gets to
fall down. It flies SIDEWAYS! Glass, zooming sideways, everywhere, all the
time.

**REX:** So if I stood there with my umbrella...

**NARRATOR:** Your umbrella would be gone. And Rex... you would be VERY shiny.

**EXPLORER:** *(giggling)* Shiny Rex!

**NARRATOR:** Fifthly — on this planet, a whole year takes only two days!
That means... a birthday every two days!

**EXPLORER:** A birthday every two days?! That's the BEST planet!

**REX:** That is TOO much cake. And I am a robot who loves cake.

**NARRATOR:** Sixthly — scientists sniffed this planet with a big space
telescope. And guess what they found? The planet smells like... rotten
eggs! Pee-yew!

**REX:** Scary AND stinky? This planet needs a bath.

**NARRATOR:** And lastly — the saddest one. The planet's sun is so close and
so hot, it is slowly melting the planet away. Little by little. Like an ice
cream cone on a very sunny day.

**EXPLORER:** But wait. Nobody has ever gone there. No rocket. No astronaut.
So how do we know ALL of this?

**NARRATOR:** Because of one tiny dot of light! When the planet passes in
front of its sun, a little bit of sunlight shines through its sky. And that
light tells us secrets. It told us about the glass. It told us about the
wind. It even told us about the stinky smell! Scientists are like
detectives — and starlight is their clue.

**REX:** Hmph. Okay. That is... actually amazing.

**NARRATOR:** So! A real planet that looks like a calm blue marble — but is
really a giant, super-fast, stinky glass storm. Nobody could make that up.
Because the real universe... is better than fiction!

**NARRATOR:** Next time — a planet as big as the BIGGEST planet you know...
but as soft and fluffy as COTTON CANDY. And yes. It is real!

**REX:** A cotton candy planet? Sure. And I'm a refrigerator.

**EXPLORER:** See you next time!

## Fact basis (all real, simple words in script)
- HD 189733b: blue color measured by Hubble (2013); blue from silicate
  (glass) cloud scattering, not water.
- Dayside ~1,000 °C ("hotter than a hundred pizza ovens").
- Winds ~8,700 km/h, faster than sound ("faster than a race car / plane / sound").
- Tidally locked ("never turns around; one side always looks at its sun").
- Sideways molten-glass rain (standard scientific characterization).
- Year = 2.2 Earth days ("birthday every two days").
- Hydrogen sulfide detected by JWST 2024 ("smells like rotten eggs").
- Atmosphere evaporating ("melting away like an ice cream cone").
- All learned via transmission spectroscopy ("secrets in starlight").
