---

**NARRATOR:** What if you could only drink soft drinks? No water. Not one
drop. Ever again.

**REX:** Finally! A dream come true. Goodbye boring water. Hello bubbles!

**EXPLORER:** Rex... I don't think you'd say that if you knew what happens next.

**NARRATOR:** And here's the thing — we don't have to guess. Your body is
real. Sugar is real. And scientists know EXACTLY what soda does inside you.
Ready? Let's go!

**NARRATOR:** Firstly — the sugar mountain. One can of soda hides ten
spoons of sugar. TEN! Nobody would ever eat ten spoons of sugar. But when
it hides in bubbles... gulp. Gone. Drink soda all day instead of water, and
you'd swallow a whole CUP of sugar. Every. Single. Day.

**REX:** A cup of sugar a day? Even I think that's too much. And I run on
batteries.

**NARRATOR:** Secondly — your teeth. Soda is not just sweet. It is SOUR
inside — full of acid. Acid is what makes lemons go zing! Now imagine your
teeth swimming in lemon-zing... all day, every day. Slowly, slowly, your
hard shiny teeth would turn soft. Like chalk.

**EXPLORER:** My teeth?! I LIKE my teeth!

**NARRATOR:** Thirdly — here's the sneaky part. Soda makes you MORE
thirsty! The sugar asks your body for extra water just to clean it up. So
you drink soda... and get thirstier... so you drink MORE soda... and get
even THIRSTIER!

**REX:** A drink that makes you thirsty? That's like a blanket that makes
you cold. Who designed this?!

**NARRATOR:** Fourthly — and this is the big one. YOU are mostly water!
More than half of you! Your blood? Mostly water. Your brain? Like a soft
water balloon! Your body is a water machine — and it wants WATER. Not
bubbles. Not sugar. Water.

**EXPLORER:** So my brain... is a water balloon?

**REX:** That explains so much.

**NARRATOR:** Fifthly — the rollercoaster. All that sugar rushes into you —
wheee, you feel super fast! And then... crash. You feel slow, and tired,
and grumpy. Up, down. Up, down. All day long.

**REX:** Sounds exhausting. I prefer my energy like my math. Steady.

**NARRATOR:** And lastly — a real space bonus! In 1985, soda actually went
to SPACE. Real astronauts tested special space cans on a real spaceship.
You know what they discovered? In space, with no gravity... the bubbles
don't float up! Burps in space are... very, very bad news.

**EXPLORER:** Ewwww!

**REX:** I have so many questions. And zero desire for answers.

**NARRATOR:** So — could you survive on only soft drinks? For a while, yes —
soda is mostly water, deep down. But your teeth would go soft, your body
would ride the sugar rollercoaster, and you'd feel thirsty ALL the time.
Water asks for nothing. No sugar, no acid, no crash. Water is the quiet
superhero of every drink.

**EXPLORER:** But wait — how do we KNOW all this?

**NARRATOR:** Because doctors and scientists measured it! Real teeth in
real experiments. Real bodies, real sugar, real results. Nobody guessed.
They checked.

**REX:** I also checked. My conclusion: water wins. Don't tell the bubbles.

**NARRATOR:** So — a drink that makes you thirstier, teeth that turn to
chalk, and burps that ruined a spaceship. Nobody made that up. Because the
real world... is better than fiction!

**NARRATOR:** Next time — back to space, and a planet as big as the BIGGEST
planet you know... but as soft and fluffy as COTTON CANDY. And yes. It is
real!

**REX:** Still not believing the cotton candy thing.

**EXPLORER:** See you next time!

## Fact basis (all real)
- ~10 tsp (39 g) sugar per 12-oz cola can.
- Phosphoric/citric acid (pH ~2.5-3.5) erodes tooth enamel — measured in
  dental studies ("teeth turn soft like chalk").
- High sugar intake increases osmotic water demand → increased thirst.
- Human body ~60% water; brain ~75% ("water balloon").
- Glucose spike/crash cycle ("rollercoaster").
- Coca-Cola and Pepsi tested dispensing cans on Space Shuttle STS-51-F
  (July 1985); carbonation behaves badly in microgravity (wet burps).
- Soda is ~90% water, so short-term survival yes, long-term harms real.
