# Better Than Fiction — Script Style Guide (LOCKED FORMAT)

Every episode script follows this format. Simple enough for a 6-year-old,
fun for all ages. This is the voice of the channel.

## The structure (every episode)
1. **Question hook** — "What if...?" premise. Robot answers with a silly joke.
2. **It's real!** — the explorer reveals the thing is REAL. Give the real
   object a fun nickname (e.g. "the Glass Storm Planet") instead of its
   scientific name.
3. **Numbered facts** — "Firstly... secondly... thirdly..." 5–7 facts.
   Each fact is one simple picture. A gag from the cast every few lines.
4. **How do we know?** — the explorer asks; the narrator gives the
   starlight-detective answer. Science = detectives, telescopes = clues.
5. **Wrap + tease** — "the real universe is better than fiction" line,
   then tease the next episode by its fun name. Robot ends on a disbelief
   gag, explorer signs off: "See you next time!"

## Language rules
- Short sentences. Nothing over ~12 words.
- ZERO science jargon. Never say: silicate, hydrogen sulfide, tidally
  locked, atmosphere, albedo, spectroscopy. Instead show the idea with
  something a child knows: pizza oven, race car, birthday cake, umbrella,
  ice cream melting in the sun.
- Numbers become feelings: not "8,700 km/h" but "faster than the fastest
  race car, faster than an airplane, faster than SOUND."
- Repetition is good: "hotter and hotter and hotter", "far, far away".
- Sound effects in narration welcome: whooooosh, plop plop plop, pee-yew!
- Every fact must still be TRUE — simple words, real science. The brand
  promise is "this is real," said at least twice per episode.

## Cast voices
- **Narrator** (percy voice): warm, excited storyteller. Asks "ready? Let's go!"
- **Explorer** (sparky voice): asks the questions a kid would ask; delighted.
- **Rex the robot** (prof voice): deadpan skeptic; silly literal jokes;
  grudging "okay, that's actually amazing" near the end.

## Length
- ~4 minutes standard (≈600 words of narration).
- Never stretch for length. If the facts run out, the episode is done.
