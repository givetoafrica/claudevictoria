# First 10 Episodes — "Sounds Fake. It's Real." (working series name: Worlds That Shouldn't Exist)

Derived from AumSum cluster analysis (data/aumsum_clusters.md): Cluster 1
(fantasy planets, ~603K avg) and Cluster 2 (space disaster stories, ~439K avg)
are the winning formats. Our differentiator: identical impossible-image
packaging, but every subject is a real astronomical object — the payoff
"this actually exists" targets family co-viewing, not made-for-kids.

## Title formulas (extracted from the data)
- Cluster 1: `The [Impossible Modifier] [Familiar Thing] [Space Noun]!`
  (single instantly-visualizable paradox image)
- Cluster 2: `The Day [Cosmic Body] [Dramatic Verb]!` / `The [Force] Storm!`
  (past-tense stakes; 2-6 minute runtime)

## Episode slate

Flagships (8-12 min, Cluster-1 pattern), weekly:
1. The Planet Where It Rains Glass — Sideways!  (HD 189733b: silicate rain, 8,700 km/h winds)
2. The Cotton Candy Planet!  (Kepler-51 super-puffs, density below styrofoam)
3. The Planet Made of Diamond!  (55 Cancri e carbon world)
4. The Planet Darker Than Coal!  (TrES-2b, <1% albedo, mechanism uncertain)
5. The Giant Eyeball Planet!  (tidally locked ice "eyeball" worlds)
6. The Mirror Planet That Shouldn't Exist!  (LTT9779b, metal clouds, highest albedo known)

Event shorts (3-6 min, Cluster-2 pattern), weekly:
7. The Day Earth Had Two Moons!  (Theia impact / Moon formation)
8. The Storm Bigger Than Earth — That Never Stops!  (Jupiter's Great Red Spot)
9. The Deadliest Magnet in the Universe!  (magnetars)
10. The Star That Could Explode Any Day!  (Betelgeuse supernova scenario)

Launch order: start with #1; alternate flagship/short (2 uploads/week for
5 weeks). Every episode ends with a spoken, named tease of the next one.

## Episode structure (every episode)
1. Cold open (0:00-0:30): show the impossible thing; thumbnail paid off before 30s.
2. The doubt (0:30-2:00): recurring cast reacts — "that can't be real."
3. Three escalations: each act weirder; visual gags for kids, dry wit for adults.
4. The reveal: the real mechanism — "scientists actually found this."
5. Fact stack (60s): three rapid "it gets weirder" facts (rewatch/share section).
6. Open loop: name and tease the next episode.

## Packaging rules
- Thumbnail: one giant object left, recurring characters reacting right,
  max 4 words of baked-in text.
- No hashtags in titles. No "kids" anywhere. Channel audience: NOT made for kids.
- One series name on all 10 episodes to build a single semantic cluster.

## Metrics after episode 10
- Primary: viewers returning within 7 days.
- Flagships: average percentage viewed >= ~50%.
- Compare flagship vs short cluster performance; double down on the winner.
